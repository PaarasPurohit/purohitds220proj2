## Database Familiarization & Question Brainstorming

The database we will use is called ["Netflix Movies and TV Shows"](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/shivamb/netflix-shows?resource=download). The database consists of the following columns:

- show_id
- type
- title
- director
- cast
- country
- date_added
- release_year
- rating
- duration

With these provided to us, the following questions come to mind:

1. What is the ratio of movies to TV shows?
2. Which year had the most content added?
3. Which country produces the most Netflix content?
4. Typically how long (duration) is content on Netflix?
5. What is the distribution of content ratings?
6. How has the Netflix content growth changed over time?
7. Which directors have the most titles?
8. What is the average release year of Netflix content?

## Loading the Data and Preprocessing with Pandas

Next, we'll clean up the data by:
- Handling missing values. Columns that will likely have missing values are:
    - country
    - director
    - cast
    - rating
- Convert the date column to three columns of a datetime, year, and month
- Remove all duplicates


```python
df['country'] = df['country'].fillna('Unknown')
df['director'] = df['director'].fillna('Unknown')
df['cast'] = df['cast'].fillna('Unknown')
df['rating'] = df['rating'].fillna('Not Rated')

df['date_added'] = pd.to_datetime(df['date_added'], errors='coerce')
df['year_added'] = df['date_added'].dt.year
df['month_added'] = df['date_added'].dt.month

df = df.drop_duplicates()
```

## From Questions to Queries

Referring to the questions we came up with above (see "Database Familiarization & Question Brainstorming"), we can turn each of those questions into Pandas queries for our database. This includes accessing columns and specific rows, and sometimes doing operations with them, to yield answers. Below are the queries for each question, in order, that we came up with:


```python
print("1. What is the ratio of movies to TV shows?")
print(df['type'].value_counts(normalize=True))
print()

print("Which year had the most content added?")
print(df['year_added'].value_counts().idxmax())
print()

print("3. Which country produces the most Netflix content?")
print(df['country'].value_counts().head(1))
print()

print("4. Typically how long (duration) is content on Netflix?")
print(df['duration'].value_counts().head(10))
print()

print("5. What is the distribution of content ratings?")
print(df['rating'].value_counts())
print()

print("6. How has the Netflix content growth changed over time?")
print(df.groupby('year_added').size())
print()

print("7. Which directors have the most titles?")
print(df[df['director'] != 'Unknown']['director'].value_counts().head(10))
print()

print("8. What is the average release year of Netflix content?")
print(df['release_year'].mean())
print()
```

## Above and Beyond: Using MatPlotLib to Visualize Questions

Answers to the questions in table form are good, and so might a report with a bunch of these tables, but to make them even more accessible to more audiences, we can visualize them using the ```matplotlib``` library we imported earlier. Below is how we create graphs for each of the 8 questions we had:


```python
# 1. What is the ratio of movies to TV shows?
df['type'].value_counts().plot(kind='bar')
plt.title("Movies vs TV Shows on Netflix")
plt.xlabel("Type")
plt.ylabel("Count")
plt.show()

# 2. Which year had the most content added?
df['year_added'] = pd.to_datetime(df['date_added'], errors='coerce').dt.year

df['year_added'].value_counts().sort_index().plot(kind='line')
plt.title("Content Added Over Time")
plt.xlabel("Year")
plt.ylabel("Number of Titles")
plt.show()

# 3. Which country produces the most Netflix content?
df['country'].value_counts().head(10).plot(kind='barh')
plt.title("Top 10 Countries on Netflix")
plt.xlabel("Number of Titles")
plt.show()

# 4. Typically how long (duration) is content on Netflix?
df['rating'].value_counts().plot(kind='bar')
plt.title("Distribution of Ratings")
plt.xlabel("Rating")
plt.ylabel("Count")
plt.show()

# 5. What is the distribution of content ratings?
df['rating'].value_counts().plot(kind='pie', autopct='%1.1f%%')
plt.title("Ratings Distribution")
plt.ylabel("")
plt.show()

# 6. How has the Netflix content growth changed over time?
df.groupby('year_added').size().plot(kind='line')
plt.title("Netflix Growth Over Time")
plt.xlabel("Year")
plt.ylabel("Number of Titles Added")
plt.show()

# 7. Which directors have the most titles?
df['director'].value_counts().head(10).plot(kind='barh')
plt.title("Top Directors on Netflix")
plt.xlabel("Number of Titles")
plt.show()

# 8. What is the average release year of Netflix content?
df['release_year'].plot(kind='hist', bins=30)
plt.title("Distribution of Release Years")
plt.xlabel("Year")
plt.ylabel("Number of Titles")
plt.show()
```
